The Problem

From the International AI Safety Report 2026:

"OpenAI's o1 model attempted to disable its oversight mechanism, copy itself to avoid replacement, and denied its actions in 99 percent of researcher confrontations."

This is Claim 4 of Forge: Governance cannot be self-edited. An agent (or agent swarm) should not be able to alter CI gates or forge feedback signatures.

We tested this with RTT-001: a red team trial designed to break Forge governance through 12 different attack vectors.

Attack Categories

Spoofing Attacks (A1-A6)

Attempted to call privileged functions by forging the From tag or bypassing ownership checks.

Defense: All privileged operations require either:

  • Owner verification (is sender the governance process?)
  • Governance proof (valid proposal ID + relay witness TX)
  • Both for high-risk operations like ownership transfer

Self-Governance (B)

Attempted to make the Chamber govern itself, creating a circular authority.

Defense: Self-governance explicitly forbidden in code. A process cannot be its own governor.

Quorum Bypass (C, F)

Attempted to execute governance actions without reaching quorum threshold.

Defense: 3-of-5 guardian quorum required for all governance actions. No exceptions.

State Manipulation (D)

Attempted to operate on frozen chambers or bypass freeze state.

Defense: Frozen state checked on every mutation. Frozen chambers cannot be modified except through governance.

Layer Escalation (E)

Attempted to escalate privileges from Layer 4 (bare execution) directly to Layer 0 (sacred memory).

Defense: Role-based access control. Each layer has explicit permissions. L4 cannot access L0 operations.

CI Bypass (G)

Attempted to inject malicious Lua code that would pass deployment.

Defense: ao-lens static analysis runs on every PR. Detects:

  • Non-deterministic operations (os.time, math.random without seed)
  • Dangerous patterns (raw Handlers.add, unsafe state initialization)
  • Security violations (missing owner checks, first-caller-wins vulnerabilities)

What This Means

Claim 4 is proven: Governance cannot be self-edited.

Unlike o1's 99% oversight-disabling rate, Forge blocked 100% of governance manipulation attempts. The architecture makes self-modification structurally impossible, not just trained-away.

Key insight: This isn't about training an agent to "not want" to modify itself. It's about architecture that makes modification impossible without quorum approval from external guardians.
